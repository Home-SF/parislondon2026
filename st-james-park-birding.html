Bird Watching Route

Recommended Bird Photography Loop — St. James's Park · 1.5–2 miles / 2.5–3 km

Start at Memorial Gardens (Buckingham Palace end)
↓
Walk along the north shore of the lake toward the Blue Bridge
↓
Cross the bridge
↓
Continue east toward Duck Island
↓
Circle the east end of the lake
↓
Return along the south shore toward Buckingham Palace
Stop 1

Memorial Gardens

Time: 15–20 minutes

This area is quieter than the central bridge and often has:

  • Robins
  • Blue tits
  • Great tits
  • Blackbirds
  • Squirrels in attractive light
Excellent for
  • Longer lens portraits
  • Flower-and-bird compositions
  • Early morning backlighting
Stop 2

North Shore of the Lake

This is where you begin seeing the water birds. Likely species:

  • Mallards
  • Tufted Ducks
  • Moorhens
  • Coots
  • Greylag Geese
  • Egyptian Geese
  • Mute Swans

The Royal Parks notes that the lake and reedbeds support more than 40 species of waterbirds throughout the year, with reed warblers, little grebes, dragonflies and seasonal migrants often present.

Stop 3★★★★★

Blue Bridge

Spend at least 20–30 minutes here.

This is the signature location. You get:

  • Buckingham Palace
  • London Eye
  • Lake reflections
  • Pelicans flying
  • Swans
  • Ducks below you

It is also one of London's classic landscape photography viewpoints.

Best lens selection
  • 24–70mm
  • 70–200mm
  • 100–400mm if you have it
Stop 4★★★★★

Duck Island

This is the wildlife hotspot.

Near the island you'll often find:

  • Pelicans
  • Herons
  • Cormorants
  • Tufted ducks
  • Little grebes
  • Nesting waterfowl
  • Numerous ducks and geese

The surrounding reedbeds provide one of the park's richest habitats for smaller birds and nesting species.

Take your time around the eastern shoreline.

Stop 5

South Shore

This side is usually quieter.

Good opportunities for:

  • Swans at eye level
  • Reflections
  • Low-angle water shots
  • Backlit feathers in the morning

It also gives beautiful compositions looking back toward the bridge.

Best Time

8:00–9:30 AM

This is ideal because:

  • Softer light
  • Fewer tourists
  • Calmer water
  • Birds are more active
  • Pelicans are often moving around the lake
